Project Description – Trader Behavior Insights

This project analyzes how Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) affects trader performance using real trading data.

The main goal was to understand whether traders behave differently during Fear and Greed market conditions, and how that impacts their profitability, risk-taking, and decision-making.

What I Did
Cleaned and processed raw trading data and sentiment data
Merged both datasets using date to align market mood with trading activity
Analyzed key metrics like:
Profit/Loss (PnL)
Trade frequency
Leverage usage
Win rate
Created visualizations to clearly compare behavior during Fear vs Greed
Key Findings
Traders generally make higher profits during Greed phases
Losses are more common during Fear periods
Traders tend to use higher leverage in Greed, increasing risk
Trading activity is higher in Greed and lower in Fear
Conclusion

Market sentiment has a strong impact on trader behavior.
Greed leads to aggressive trading and higher risk, while Fear results in cautious behavior and lower participation.

Understanding this relationship can help traders make more disciplined and data-driven decisions instead of emotional ones.
